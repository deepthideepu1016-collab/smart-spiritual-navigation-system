Darshan Information

Tiruvannamalai Arunachaleswarar Temple provides different darshan facilities for devotees visiting Lord Shiva. Thousands of devotees visit daily and lakhs of devotees gather during Pournami and Karthigai Deepam festivals.


  • Free Darshan – Available for all devotees.
  • ₹50 Special Darshan Line – Faster queue access.
  • Festival Special Darshan available during important occasions.
  • Separate arrangements for senior citizens and differently-abled devotees.
  • Early morning darshan is considered spiritually powerful and peaceful.

Rajagopuram

The Rajagopuram of Tiruvannamalai Temple is one of the tallest temple towers in South India and stands as the grand entrance to the sacred temple.


Built during the Vijayanagara period, the tower reflects magnificent Dravidian architecture with beautiful carvings, sculptures and sacred designs.


Devotees believe entering through the Rajagopuram symbolizes entering a divine spiritual path toward inner peace and enlightenment.


The Eastern Rajagopuram rises to approximately 217 feet with 11 magnificent tiers.

Agni Lingam

Agni Lingam is one among the sacred Ashta Lingams situated around the Girivalam path of Arunachala Hill. It represents the fire element and the powerful energy of Lord Shiva.


Devotees pray at Agni Lingam seeking courage, purification, positivity and spiritual strength. Many pilgrims stop here during Girivalam and offer prayers and deepam.

Temple History

Tiruvannamalai Arunachaleswarar Temple is one of the oldest and most sacred Shiva temples in India. It is one among the Pancha Bhoota Sthalams representing the fire element.


According to Hindu mythology, Lord Shiva appeared here as an infinite column of fire before Lord Vishnu and Lord Brahma.


The temple has been expanded and maintained by great dynasties including the Cholas, Pandyas and Vijayanagara kings.

Special Pujas & Festivals

Tiruvannamalai Temple celebrates many grand spiritual festivals throughout the year with great devotion.


  • Karthigai Deepam – Sacred flame lighting festival on Arunachala Hill.
  • Pournami Girivalam – Lakhs of devotees walk around Arunachala Hill.
  • Maha Shivaratri – Night-long Shiva worship and abhishekams.
  • Daily Abhishekam and Deeparadhana rituals inside the temple.
