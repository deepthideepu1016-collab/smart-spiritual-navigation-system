🗺 Girivalam Route Map

Tiruvannamalai Girivalam is a sacred 14 KM walking route around Arunachala Hill. Devotees walk through the Girivalam path and visit the 8 Ashta Lingams for spiritual blessings. This route helps devotees complete Girivalam with proper direction and guidance.

🕉 8 Ashta Lingams Information

🕉 1. Indra Lingam

Location: Located on the eastern side of Arunachala Hill, approximately 1.5 KM from Arunachaleswarar Temple.

Significance:
Indra Lingam is dedicated to Lord Indra, the King of the Devas. It is the first Ashta Lingam visited during Girivalam. Devotees believe that worshipping here removes obstacles, improves leadership qualities, brings prosperity and earns divine blessings.

Spiritual Benefits:

  • ✔ Success in career and business
  • ✔ Leadership qualities
  • ✔ Prosperity and wealth
  • ✔ Divine blessings
  • ✔ Peace of mind

Best Time to Visit: Early morning during Girivalam or Pournami.

Nearby Facilities: Drinking water point, resting area, Anna Prasadam centre and public toilet facilities may be available nearby depending on local arrangements.

🔥 2. Agni Lingam

Location: Situated in the south-east direction of Arunachala Hill on the Girivalam path.

Significance:
Agni Lingam represents the fire element. Devotees pray here for purification of mind, courage, strength and removal of negative thoughts. This Lingam is spiritually connected with energy, light and inner transformation.

Spiritual Benefits:

  • ✔ Removes negative energy
  • ✔ Gives courage and confidence
  • ✔ Improves spiritual strength
  • ✔ Purifies the mind
  • ✔ Brings positive energy

Best Time to Visit: Morning or evening during Girivalam.

Nearby Facilities: Water points, devotional shops, resting space and public facilities may be available near this area.

⚖️ 3. Yama Lingam

Location: Located on the southern side of the Girivalam route.

Significance:
Yama Lingam is associated with Lord Yama, who represents discipline, justice and dharma. Devotees worship here for good health, long life, protection from fear and strength to live a righteous life.

Spiritual Benefits:

  • ✔ Long life and good health
  • ✔ Protection from fear
  • ✔ Self-discipline
  • ✔ Mental strength
  • ✔ Peaceful life

Best Time to Visit: Early morning is preferred.

Nearby Facilities: Drinking water, rest areas, food stalls and toilet facilities may be available at selected public points.

🛡️ 4. Niruthi Lingam

Location: Situated in the south-west direction of Arunachala Hill.

Significance:
Niruthi Lingam is believed to protect devotees from negative energies, evil influences and difficulties. Worshipping here gives courage, stability and protection during challenging times.

Spiritual Benefits:

  • ✔ Protection from negative energy
  • ✔ Removal of obstacles
  • ✔ Mental peace
  • ✔ Family harmony
  • ✔ Spiritual protection

Best Time to Visit: Morning or evening.

Nearby Facilities: Water points, resting areas and Anna Prasadam service may be available during Pournami and festival days.

🌊 5. Varuna Lingam

Location: Located on the western side of Arunachala Hill.

Significance:
Varuna Lingam represents the water element. Devotees pray here for purity, peace, emotional balance and family harmony. This Lingam is believed to bring calmness and remove emotional disturbances.

Spiritual Benefits:

  • ✔ Emotional balance
  • ✔ Family happiness
  • ✔ Peace of mind
  • ✔ Purity and calmness
  • ✔ Spiritual peace

Best Time to Visit: Any time during Girivalam.

Nearby Facilities: Drinking water, resting areas, medical help access and public toilet facilities may be available near the main road.

🌬️ 6. Vayu Lingam

Location: Situated in the north-west direction of Arunachala Hill.

Significance:
Vayu Lingam represents the air element and life energy. Devotees worship here for good health, breathing strength, peace and positive energy. It is considered helpful for spiritual focus and meditation.

Spiritual Benefits:

  • ✔ Good health
  • ✔ Positive energy
  • ✔ Peaceful mind
  • ✔ Better breathing and vitality
  • ✔ Spiritual strength

Best Time to Visit: Morning hours are preferred.

Nearby Facilities: Water points, rest areas, devotional shops and public facilities may be available along the route.

💰 7. Kubera Lingam

Location: Located on the northern side of Arunachala Hill.

Significance:
Kubera Lingam is connected with Lord Kubera, the deity of wealth and prosperity. Devotees worship here for financial stability, success in business, growth and abundance in life.

Spiritual Benefits:

  • ✔ Financial growth
  • ✔ Business success
  • ✔ Prosperity
  • ✔ Good fortune
  • ✔ Family happiness

Best Time to Visit: Morning or evening.

Nearby Facilities: Drinking water, Anna Prasadam service areas, rest points and toilet facilities may be available nearby.

🕉️ 8. Esanya Lingam

Location: Located on the north-east side of Arunachala Hill, close to the completion of the Girivalam route.

Significance:
Esanya Lingam is associated with wisdom, spiritual growth and moksha. It is the final Ashta Lingam visited during Girivalam. Devotees pray here for inner peace, knowledge, devotion and liberation.

Spiritual Benefits:

  • ✔ Wisdom and knowledge
  • ✔ Spiritual growth
  • ✔ Inner peace
  • ✔ Moksha
  • ✔ Divine blessings

Best Time to Visit: Early morning or after completing Girivalam.

Nearby Facilities: Drinking water, rest area, temple shops and easy return access towards Arunachaleswarar Temple.

💧 General Facilities on Girivalam Route

💧 Drinking Water Points

Drinking water points are available at different places along the Girivalam route to support devotees during the 14 KM walk.

🪑 Rest Points

Resting areas are useful for elderly devotees, children and pilgrims who need short breaks during the Girivalam journey.

🍛 Anna Prasadam Centers

Anna Prasadam service is commonly available during Pournami, Girivalam days and festival days through temple and local service arrangements.

🙏 Free Rudraksha Points

Some spiritual service stalls and devotional centres may provide Rudraksha, spiritual guidance and support to pilgrims.

Note: Facility availability may change depending on crowd, Pournami days, festival days and local arrangements. Devotees should also check nearby signboards and local help points during Girivalam.
